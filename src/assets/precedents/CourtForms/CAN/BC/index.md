# British Columbia Court Forms - Document Discovery
Last updated: 2025-11-17

## Overview

British Columbia uses a **List of Documents** (Form 22) as its primary discovery document under Supreme Court Civil Rules, Rule 7-1. This form must be served within 35 days after the end of the pleading period and contains a 4-part structure for listing documents.

## 1. Form 22 - List of Documents (Official Blank Template)

**Document Type**: Primary discovery document (official blank template)

**URL**: https://www2.gov.bc.ca/assets/gov/law-crime-and-justice/courthouse-services/court-files-records/court-forms/supreme-civil/22.pdf

**Source**: BC Government - Ministry of Attorney General (official)

**Description**: Official blank template for BC Supreme Court Form 22 (List of Documents) under Supreme Court Civil Rules. This is the primary discovery document in BC, required to be served within 35 days after the end of the pleading period.

**Four-Part Structure**:
- Part 1: Documents that could prove/disprove material facts
- Part 2: Other documents to be referred to at trial
- Part 3: Documents that relate to matters in question
- Part 4: Privileged documents

**Rule Citation**: Supreme Court Civil Rules, Rule 7-1

**Suggested Filename**: `CANBC-Form22-ListOfDocuments-template.pdf`

## Case Law with Discovery Document Discussions

The following BC cases provide judicial interpretation of List of Documents requirements under Rule 7-1:

1. **Biehl v. Strang**, 2010 BCSC 1391
   - Defines "material fact" for Part 1 document listing
   - Discusses scope of disclosure obligations

2. **Jones v. Donaghey**, 2011 BCCA 6
   - BC Court of Appeal decision on discovery scope
   - Addresses privileged documents (Part 4)

3. **More Marine Ltd. v. Shearwater Marine Ltd.**
   - Discovery obligations under new Supreme Court Civil Rules
   - Practical application of 4-part structure

4. **Edwards v. Ganzer**, 2012 BCSC 138
   - Document production requirements
   - Ongoing disclosure obligations under Rule 7-1(9)

**Access**: Case law available free on CanLII.org; actual court filings (including filed Lists of Documents) available through BC Court Services Online ($6/document)

## Jurisdictional Notes

### Terminology
- **"List of Documents"** - BC's term for discovery document listing (Form 22)
- **NOT** called "Affidavit of Documents" in BC (used in Ontario, Saskatchewan, Manitoba, etc.)
- **NOT** called "Initial Disclosures" (US federal term)

### Key Rules
- **Rule 7-1**: Primary discovery rule governing Lists of Documents
- **Rule 7-7**: Notice to Admit documents and facts
- **Timing**: 35 days after end of pleading period
- **Ongoing Obligation**: Rule 7-1(9) requires amended lists when new documents obtained

### Document Discovery
1. **List of Documents** (Form 22) - within 35 days
2. **Appointment to Examine** (Form 23) - schedule discovery examination
3. **Notice to Admit** (Form 26) - request admissions (optional but strategic)
4. **Examination for Discovery** - oral questioning phase
